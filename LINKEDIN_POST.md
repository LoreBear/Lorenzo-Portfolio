Psychologist's Take: These aren't isolated incidents—they're symptoms of broader organizational dynamics. As Hobfoll's (1989) Conservation of Resources theory explains, when work fails to provide sufficient resources for recovery, disengagement occurs regardless of satisfaction levels. The psychological contract—the unwritten expectations between employee and employer—is the invisible architecture of employment relationships.

Business Meaning: This diagnostic approach enables precise resource allocation to high-risk areas rather than blanket interventions. By applying frameworks like my OJT (Overtime workload management, Job satisfaction enhancement, Enhanced onboarding) and S.A.F.E. (Supervisor focus, Access transparency, Fear reduction, Equity for remote workers), HR can shift from reactive incident response to evidence-based risk stratification—answering "who is at risk, and what does intervention cost?"
