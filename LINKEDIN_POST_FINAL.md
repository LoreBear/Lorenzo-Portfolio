Been sitting at the intersection of org psych and data analytics lately, trying to move past basic HR reporting to find what actually drives how people behave at work.

Here's what jumped out:

IBM HR Analysis: People working lots of overtime leave at 30.5% - nearly 3x more than those with normal hours.

DEI Dashboard EMEA: We've got 33.9% women overall, but they're leaving at 20.4% vs 15.3% for men. So for every guy who leaves, 1.33 women walk out the door.

OSMI Mental Health Tech: Only 4 in 10 feel safe talking to bosses about mental health. Over 60% worry it'll hurt their career.

Psychologist's Take: This isn't random turnover - it's the organization talking. When work drains people faster than it fills them up (thanks, Hobfoll 1989), they check out. That "psychological contract" thing - the unwritten boss-employee deal - matters more than we think.

Business Meaning: Instead of shooting in the dark with blanket programs, we can aim where it counts. My OJT (overtime, joy, onboarding) and S.A.F.E. (bosses, access, no fear, remote equity) frameworks help HR shift from band-aids to real fixes - answering who's at risk and what helping them actually costs.
